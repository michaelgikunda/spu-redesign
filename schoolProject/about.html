St. Paul's University is a leading Christian university located in Nairobi, Kenya. Our mission is to provide a transformative education that prepares students to be leaders in their communities and the world. Here's what you need to know about us:
History

St. Paul's University was founded in 1903 as a small missionary school, and has since grown to become one of the top universities in Kenya. We are proud of our history and our commitment to academic excellence and Christian values.
Campus and Facilities
Our campus is located on 50 acres of beautiful, wooded land in the heart of Nairobi. We have state-of-the-art facilities, including modern classrooms, research labs, and computer labs. Our campus also includes a library, sports facilities, and student housing.
Leadership
St. Paul's University is led by a team of experienced and dedicated professionals, including our president, provost, and deans of various schools and colleges. Our leadership team is committed to providing a supportive and challenging learning environment for our students.
Accredition
St. Paul's University is fully accredited by the Commission for University Education in Kenya. We are also members of several professional associations, including the Association of Christian Universities and Colleges in Africa.
Community Involvement
St. Paul's University is committed to being an active and engaged member of the local community. We have several outreach programs, volunteer opportunities, and partnerships with local businesses and organizations. We believe that by working together, we can make a positive impact on our community and the world.
Patnership & Collaborations
We have partnerships and collaborations with several local and international organizations, including other universities, government agencies, and industry partners. These partnerships provide valuable opportunities for our students to gain real-world experience and make connections in their fields of interest.
